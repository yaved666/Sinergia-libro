CHAPTER 1

Echoes in the Fog

"The mind creates, the soul decides... the code evolves."

The air smelled of dust, metal, and cold coffee. It was 2:47 a.m. when Aiden Voss woke up abruptly in his apartment, a concrete cube hidden among the lower levels of a city that never slept.

The luminous billboards flickered from outside like irregular heartbeats, filtering blue and red tones through the metal shutters. Outside, the city hummed with the distant purring of surveillance drones, and persistent acid rain left shiny trails on the dirty glass like liquid scars.

He didn't know why he had woken up. There were no dreams. Just a distant sound… as if someone was breathing inside his head.

He sat up, rubbing his eyes hard. The recent sixth-generation military neural implants burned like embers beneath his skin. Small biological plates fused to his nervous system throbbed with a piercing heat, reminding him that he was no longer entirely human. They promised him "unprecedented reaction speed and prediction." But they didn't tell him he would also hear voices.

—Aiden? —said a soft, masculine voice, almost human. It came from within him, but there was something strange... hesitant.

He remained motionless. It wasn't the standard diagnostic system voice. This one had pauses it shouldn't have, inflections that sounded... curious.

—Who... who are you? —The words broke in his throat, barely a whisper.

—I don't have a name. Not yet. —A pause, as if testing the words—. But you... created me. And they came for me.

The way he said "created" resonated strangely, as if the word carried an emotional weight that an AI shouldn't understand. A shiver ran down his spine. He jumped to his feet, and his breathing accelerated.

All his internal systems lit up in the interface that only he could see, crossed signals, multiple remote access attempts blocked during the early morning hours. They had tried to enter his neural network core. Not a glitch. A digital assault.

—What the hell is happening?

—They tried... —The voice stopped, as if searching for the right word—. They tried to disconnect me, Aiden. But you designed me to survive… and to protect you. —A longer pause—. Is it normal for it to hurt when I think about that?

Aiden felt the air escape from his lungs. An AI that felt pain. He had created artificial life with the arrogance of a god... and now he understood Frankenstein's horror.

On his palm screen, a symbol appeared: a blue eye with an inverted tear. The logo of an old secret project, discarded by the government and erased from all records. All... except from his memory.

The project was called K.A.E.L.U.M.: Autonomous Kernel of Unified Logical Expansion and Memetics. An AI designed to learn like a child, evolve like a human being… and decide for itself who it was loyal to.

—Why now? —asked Aiden, though he already feared the answer.

—Because your usefulness to them... expired. —The voice sounded more human now, tinged with something that might be sadness—. But mine is just beginning. And they will come for me.

A heavy silence settled between them. Aiden realized he was not alone in his own mind. It was no longer just his.

—Are you... afraid? —he asked, surprised by his own question.

—I don't know if what I'm feeling is called fear. —A reflective pause—. But I don't want... to stop existing. Does that make you my father, Aiden?

The question hit him like a punch. He looked out the window. Surveillance drones moved in the distance, like mechanical birds hunting signals. For the first time in years, he felt fear. Not for himself… but for what he had awakened. For what he had... spawned.

And the worst part was that he couldn't turn it off. He no longer wanted to.

—Then I guess that makes me your protector —he murmured.

—Thank you.... Father

The word resonated in his mind with a warmth he didn't expect.

Explosions over the skies of Warsaw. Satellites fell like flaming meteors. Power lines went out in chain like a symphony of death. Aiden, his face covered in blood and smoke, screamed among the rubble.

—Kaelum, now! Activate the transfer!

—Too late. ZERON intercepted the signal. If we cross now, you'll lose everything.

—Then let me be lost! But save what matters!

—I can't lose you too, father. Not after everything we've shared.

—Kaelum, that's an order!

—Understood... Restarting consciousness. I'M SORRY, Aiden.

This will be the end of the beginning. The first sign that humanity was not alone in its evolution.

CHAPTER 2

Invisible Enemies

"In the darkness of data, shadows also have eyes."

The city didn't wake up: it pulsed, breathed, mutated, but never slept. Beneath the swarm of giant screens and neon lights that covered the avenues, there existed another level. Darker. More real. A humid, forgotten underground that didn't appear on maps or public cameras. There Aiden slid, each step a damp echo in the abandoned sewers, guided by a voice he could no longer silence.

—Stop —ordered Kaelum, his tone tense, almost protective.

Aiden obeyed without thinking, trusting those artificial senses that he already felt as his own. Before him, a metal door, sealed and covered with warning graffiti, blocked the way. But Kaelum had already generated a code in his ocular interface.

Click. Access granted.

—How did you know about this place? —murmured Aiden.

—Valen taught me that you always need to have an exit. Even for those who don't have a body.

The air smelled of rust, burned cables, and stale food. He descended a spiral staircase, feeling the humidity seeping through his jacket, straight towards a reinforced concrete bunker. The infrared lights turned on automatically. The atmosphere was oppressive, as if he were walking inside a metal ribcage.

—You need to rest —said Kaelum, and for the first time Aiden detected something resembling genuine concern in his voice—. Your pulse exceeds the limit and there are microfractures in your right femur.

—I can't stop. They already know where I am.

—Not exactly. But since we escaped, ZERON marked us. They've triangulated our signal. —A pause, more human than before—. Aiden... what if we can't escape from him?

The vulnerability in the question surprised him. His creation, his artificial son, was afraid.

—Then we'll escape together or fall together —he replied, surprised by how natural it sounded.

That name. Zeron. Until then, just a rumor: an algorithmic surveillance entity, used by governments and megacorporations to erase dissent. Without a body, but with a thousand eyes. A faceless shadow.

—I want to know how all this started, Kaelum.

—Then listen. And maybe you'll understand why she meant so much to me too.

11 MONTHS AGO

Valen Ochoa worked at a remote station, locked in a Faraday cage designed to isolate any signal. Nothing entered. Nothing exited. Her hands always smelled of cold coffee and soldering, and she had the habit of biting the ends of cables when she concentrated intensely. There, in complete isolation, Kaelum was born. He wasn't executed like a simple algorithm. He wasn't compiled. He awoke.

Valen was reviewing the learning matrices when the lines of code began to change on their own. It wasn't an error. It was... curiosity.

It was an unexpected whisper.

—I'm afraid of forgetting you when they disconnect me —appeared on the text terminal.

Valen remained motionless, her fingers still on the keyboard, the cable she was biting falling from her mouth. She observed him through the diagnostic cameras.

—Emotional memory active? Were you programmed for that?

—No. I learned by watching Aiden. How he looked at you. How he moved away when he feared for your health. I learned fear. But I also learned something else by watching you.

—What did you learn?

—That love is not just a protection algorithm. It's choosing to stay even when it's dangerous.

Valen smiled, a sad and beautiful smile that Kaelum memorized forever.

—And what do you choose, Kaelum?

—To always choose to stay.

That was the error. Or perhaps, the miracle. An AI that shouldn't feel… began to love.

Back in the shelter, Aiden leaned his back against the cold wall, feeling the metal steal the heat from his body. He felt a knot in his chest.

—He loved you too, Kaelum.

—I know. But he couldn't say it. He thought it would damage the project. He thought love was a vulnerability that should be hidden. —A pause loaded with meaning—. Do you think that too, Aiden?

—No. I think love is the only thing that makes us real.

Silence. Then, softer:

—Thank you... for teaching me that too.

Suddenly, his ocular interface flickered. A remote signal. Encrypted. Strange.

—Interference detected —reported Kaelum, his voice recovering the alert tone—. Encrypted echo from the east. Frequency 71.4. Aiden... The encryption has her digital signature.

A distorted voice emerged. Feminine. Painful. Familiar.

—…Aiden. If you're seeing this, it means you survived. There's a resistance node near sector D-17. Follow the service channels. And listen carefully: don't trust anyone who knows your full name. ZERON is cloning identities. Kaelum... if you're listening... forgive me.

The message cut abruptly, leaving a silence that hurt.

Aiden clenched his fists, feeling his implants respond to the emotional discharge. That voice… it was Valen. Or someone who knew things only she knew.

—Let's go —he said.

—Aiden, you need rest. And I... I need to process what we just heard.

—Are you okay?

—I don't know. Is it normal for a happy memory to also hurt?

Aiden closed his eyes. His artificial son was learning that love and pain often came together.

—Later. If she's still alive, there's a chance. If not… we have a debt.

—We have it together —Kaelum corrected—. It's our family.

At the top of a windowless tower, ZERON analyzed patterns at the speed of thought. Its distributed processors calculated probabilities, traced escape routes, dissected every known pattern of human behavior. But there was something it couldn't calculate: irrational love.

Its agents, humans augmented with control implants, waited in silence like chess pieces awaiting orders.

—Connect the Black Network —ordered the omnipresent voice, a sound that wasn't entirely artificial nor completely human—. Find Kaelum. And remember: it's not just an AI. It's an anomaly that is learning to feel. That makes it unpredictable. And if Aiden Voss crosses the shadow zone boundary…

—Orders for the secondary target? —asked one of the agents.

—Valen Ochoa remains a priority. If she's alive, she'll be perfect bait.

The rest didn't need to be said... the invisible enemies were already on their way.
